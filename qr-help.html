Overview

In the QR Attendance app, QR codes are crucial in tracking attendance for school events and classes.

Online Mode Attendance:

1. The administrator can delegate someone to help scan the QR codes by providing them with an attendance code that they can join in. The authorized person can now help on scanning QR Codes.

To join as a scanner, click the + button, then Join Attendance.

2. Only the attendance administrator will have access to the full attendance contents.

3. Other people assigned as scanners will only see the QR code that they have scanned. This ensures privacy and prevents unauthorized access to entire attendance records.

Scanning QR Code:

The scanner's border will initially be blue. It will change to green once a valid QR Code is scanned, and turn red if the QR Code format does not conform to the expected format. See QR Codes below for more information.

When a valid QR code is successfully scanned, the scanner will pause the scanned QR code in the frame. To continue scanning, please tap the screen.

Exporting Attendance:

The contents of the attendance can be exported to an Excel file. The exported Excel file can be found in the Downloads and then in the "Attendance" folder of the phone's storage.

Scanned QR codes that are past the set Cutoff time in the attendance would have a red background color in its Date and Time Scanned field in the excel file.

For online attendances:

The attendance administrator can export the entire contents of the attendance, but participants can only export the QR codes they scanned.

QR Codes:

The QR Codes accepted by the app are either those generated by the app itself or those that adhere to the format of ID Number&Fullname&Department. The QR Codes can be created in Offline Mode, which can be saved to phone storage in the Downloads inside the QRCodes folder.

To create multiple QR Code for your participants, you can head to a third-party website such as https://qrbatch.com
